Ancient forts in jharkhand

Some prominent ancient forts in Jharkhand include the Palamu Forts (Purana Quila and Naya Quila), built by the Chero dynasty in the 16th and 17th centuries within the Palamu Tiger Reserve. Other notable sites are the ruined Site of Old Fort of Ruam in Singhbhum, featuring mounds and remains of copper smelting, and the Kurumbera Fort in West Bengal, a medieval protected monument with temples and quarters.

1.Palamu Forts- Purana Quila And Naya Quila


The Palamu Forts, comprising Purana Quila and Naya Quila, are located within the Palamu Tiger Reserve in Jharkhand. Built by the Chero dynasty in the 16th and 17th centuries, these forts showcase impressive architecture with massive stone walls, bastions, and gateways. The forts served as strategic military outposts and offer panoramic views of the surrounding forested landscape. Visitors can explore the ruins, including temples and water tanks, while enjoying the rich history and natural beauty of the area.

Highlights:

  • Historical significance as military outposts of the Chero dynasty
  • Impressive stone architecture with bastions and gateways
  • Scenic views of the Palamu Tiger Reserve
  • Ruins featuring temples and water tanks
  • Opportunities for wildlife spotting in the surrounding forest

2. Navratangarh Fort, Gumla District


Navratangarh Fort, located in the Gumla district of Jharkhand, is a historic fort built in the 17th century by the Nagvanshi dynasty. The fort is perched on a hilltop, offering panoramic views of the surrounding landscape. It features impressive stone walls, bastions, and gateways, showcasing the architectural prowess of the era. Visitors can explore the ruins of the fort, including temples and water tanks, while enjoying the serene environment and rich history of the region.

Highlights:

  • 17th-century fort built by the Nagvanshi dynasty
  • Hilltop location with panoramic views
  • Impressive stone architecture with bastions and gateways
  • Ruins featuring temples and water tanks
  • Serene environment for exploration and photography
