“Explore the Vibrant Markets of Jharkhand”

Jharkhand is not only rich in natural beauty but also in its local markets. From traditional crafts and jewelry to fresh produce and street food, these markets reflect the culture, traditions, and daily life of the people. Exploring these markets is a treat for tourists and locals alike.

Upper Bazaar – Heart of Ranchi


Located in the center of Ranchi, Upper Bazaar is a bustling market offering traditional Paitkar paintings, tribal jewelry, apparel, and electronics. The vibrant atmosphere, local eateries, and variety of goods make it a must-visit shopping destination.

Highlights:

  • Famous for tribal crafts and jewelry.
  • Blend of traditional and modern shopping.
  • Local street food and eateries.
  • Ideal for cultural souvenirs.

Sakchi Market – Commercial Hub of Jamshedpur


Sakchi Market, near the Tata Steel plant, offers a wide range of clothing, electronics, and groceries. The weekly Mangal Bazar adds to the charm, providing visitors with a traditional market experience.

Highlights:

  • Main commercial hub of Jamshedpur.
  • Weekly traditional market (Mangal Bazar).
  • Variety of products from modern to local goods.
  • Popular for daily shopping and essentials.

Bistupur Market – Old Meets Modern


One of the oldest markets in Jamshedpur, Bistupur Market combines traditional shopping experiences with modern stores. Visitors can find clothing, accessories, and household items in a bustling environment.

Highlights:

  • Historical market with modern shops.
  • Wide range of apparel and household items.
  • Popular with locals and tourists alike.

New Market – Trendy Shopping in Ranchi


New Market, near Firayalal Chowk, offers trendy clothing, footwear, electronics, and street food. It’s a lively place to shop, enjoy local snacks, and experience Ranchi’s urban market culture.

Highlights:

  • Mix of modern stores and street vendors.
  • Famous for street food stalls.
  • Ideal for shopping essentials and fashion items.

Palash Mart – Supporting Rural Women


Palash Mart, an initiative by the Jharkhand government, empowers rural women by selling products like mustard oil, rice, honey, and bamboo handicrafts. It’s a model for sustainable development and cultural promotion.

Highlights:

  • Promotes rural women’s entrepreneurship.
  • Products include handicrafts, food items, and more.
  • Recognized for its socio-economic impact.
