“Whispers of Falling Waters”

Jharkhand is home to some of the most enchanting waterfalls in India. Surrounded by lush forests and rocky landscapes, these cascading streams create a perfect escape for nature lovers and travelers alike. From serene hidden gems to majestic roaring falls, Jharkhand’s waterfalls offer breathtaking views, adventure opportunities, and a chance to experience nature at its purest.

Lodh Waterfall– Jharkhand’s Tallest Cascade



Nestled deep within the forests of the Latehar district, Lodha Waterfall (also known as Lodh Falls) is one of the most stunning natural wonders of Jharkhand. Falling from a height of 143 meters (469 ft), it is the highest waterfall in Jharkhand and the 21st highest in India. Fed by the Burha River, the waterfall roars through rocky cliffs and dense greenery inside the Palamau Tiger Reserve, creating a breathtaking sight and a soothing natural symphony. The sound of the gushing water can be heard from several kilometers away, making it an unforgettable experience for every visitor. Located about 60 km from Netarhat and nearly 195 km from Ranchi, Lodha Waterfall is not just a scenic spot but also a paradise for adventure seekers, nature lovers, and photographers. The surrounding forests, rich wildlife, and serene atmosphere add to its charm, making it one of the must-visit attractions of Jharkhand.

Highlights:

  • Highest waterfall in Jharkhand (143 m).
  • Situated inside Palamau Tiger Reserve.
  • Best visited during monsoon and post-monsoon (July–December).
  • Popular for nature walks, photography, and peaceful escapes.

Hundru Falls – The Roaring Beauty of Jharkhand


Hundru Falls, one of the most famous waterfalls of Jharkhand, is located on the Subarnarekha River, about 45 km from Ranchi. With a dramatic drop of nearly 98 meters (320 ft), it is the highest waterfall of the Subarnarekha River and a major attraction for tourists. Surrounded by lush greenery and rocky cliffs, the waterfall forms a natural pool at its base, making it a popular spot for picnics, bathing, and photography. During the monsoon season, Hundru roars in full force, creating a breathtaking view, while in winter, the calm pool below becomes an inviting site for relaxation.

Highlights

  • Height: ~98 meters (320 ft)
  • Located ~45 km from Ranchi city
  • Best visited during monsoon (July–September) for powerful flow, and winter (Nov–Feb) for picnics.
  • A natural pool at the base is popular with visitors.
  • Surrounded by scenic trekking trails and forests.

Dassam Falls – The Thunder of Nature


Dassam Falls, located around 40 km from Ranchi on the Kanchi River, is one of the most popular waterfalls of Jharkhand. With a drop of about 44 meters (144 ft), it offers a spectacular view of water gushing down amidst dense forests and rocky landscapes. The name Dassam is derived from the local Mundari word "Da-song", meaning the act of pouring water. The falls are especially breathtaking during the monsoon season, when the river is full and the water rushes down with thunderous force.

Highlights

  • Height: ~44 meters (144 ft)
  • Located about 40 km from Ranchi, easily accessible by road.
  • Surrounded by thick forests and rocky terrain.
  • Most spectacular during monsoon when water flow is strongest
  • Popular spot for photography and picnics
